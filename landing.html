Track, Analyze, and Improve Your Practice

PracticeTrack helps musicians of all levels organize their practice sessions, set goals, and track progress with powerful tools designed for musical growth.

PracticeTrack is free. No subscription or account required. No paywalls.

Smart Practice Tools

Practice Timer

Track your practice time with automatic session saving. Add notes and categorize your practice for better organization.

Progress Tracking

Visualize your practice history in the Stats tab with detailed statistics like total time, session frequency, consistency streaks, and category breakdowns. Monitor improvements, identify trends, and discover your calculated Practice Level (based on total practice time) with explanations on how it's determined.

Goal Setting

Set daily, weekly, and monthly practice goals to stay motivated. Track your progress towards achieving milestones and see your overall goal completion rate.

Built-in Metronome

Practice with precision using our customizable metronome. Adjust tempo and time signatures to suit your needs.

Media Library

Save photos, videos, and notes of your playing to review later. Capture important moments from your practice sessions or record teacher demonstrations during lessons.

Practice Resources

Access practice categories for your instrument and guidance material on how to practice effectively, available in the Resources tab.

Informed by Music Education Research

PracticeTrack's design incorporates established music education research. Guidance materials on how to practice effectively and the app's category-based approach to structuring sessions are informed by these principles, helping you practice more efficiently.

Install PracticeTrack on Your Device

PracticeTrack is a Progressive Web App (PWA). Add it to your home screen for an app-like experience without visiting an app store.

While PracticeTrack works in any modern browser on desktop or mobile, it's optimized and recommended for use on your primary mobile device. Note that on desktop versions, direct camera capture for photos/videos is unavailable, but you can still upload existing media files. Since data is stored locally per device (no accounts), you can use the export feature within the app on your phone to transfer your data if you wish to use PracticeTrack on desktop or another device.

Android (Chrome)

  1. Launch PracticeTrack in Chrome using a button above.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu icon (⋮).
  3. Click "Install app".
  4. Follow prompts to add the icon.

iOS (Safari)

  1. Launch PracticeTrack in Safari using a button above.
  2. Tap the Share icon ( ).
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen".
  4. Confirm name ("PracticeTrack") and tap "Add".
  5. The icon will appear on your home screen.

A Journey with PracticeTrack

Mike, a guitarist, usually practices 5 or 6 days a week for about 30-60 minutes per day. He fits sessions in when he can, sometimes having more than one per day, and wants to practice effectively. His teacher recommends PracticeTrack, and they spend part of a lesson setting up relevant practice categories like E String Root Barre Chords, Alternate Picking Technique, Intervals of the Major Scale, "Mike's Metal Song" (song mike is writing), Chromatic Scale Exercises, Em Pentatonic Position 1.

See how PracticeTrack can integrate into a practice routine.

Week 1: Goals & Setup

Set Goals: 'Master E String Root Barre Chords' & 'Spend 2 hrs this week on Barre Chords'. Took a photo in his lesson of the correct hand position for reference. Logged 8 sessions across 6 days (total 2 hrs 15 min) using Timer, focusing on 'Barre Chords' drills and ticked off the 'Spend 2 hrs this week on Barre Chords' goal as complete in the Goals tab.

Week 2: Building Routine

Logged 7 sessions (total 2 hrs 30 min). Checked Stats and confirmed he met the 2-hour goal for 'Barre Chords'! Also introduced 'Em Pentatonic position 1' and saved the YouTube video link & notes in the Media tab.

Week 3: First Insights

Logged 8 sessions (total 2 hrs 45 min). Focused heavily on alternate picking; set new Goal: 'Clean Alternate Picking at 100bpm'. Marked 'Master E String Root Barre Chords' skill goal complete after dedicated practice. Recorded video of clean chord changes.

Week 4: Goal Achieved!

Logged 9 sessions (total 3 hrs 30 min). Reviewed completed 'Barre Chords' skill goal. PracticeTrack made it easy to tell his teacher exactly what he practiced and for how long.

Week 5: New Focus

Logged 10 sessions (total 4 hrs). Set new Goal: 'Understand Intervals'. Took a photo of a whiteboard illustration from his lesson for study. Maintained higher practice duration; Stats tab shows his Practice Level is now 'Medium'.

Week 6: Analyzing Trends

Logged 9 sessions (total 3 hrs 45 min). Reviewed Stats and saw less time on 'Intervals of the Major Scale' than planned. Marked 'Clean Alternate Picking at 100bpm' goal complete! Adjusted focus for the following week to ensure better balance with theory and chromatic scale exercises.

And how much time did Mike spend using PracticeTrack each day? Typically just a minute or two to start the timer, log notes, or check stats – the focus stays on the music!

Over these 6 weeks, Mike logged 51 sessions totaling nearly 19 hours. He completed 3 out of 4 goals set during this period. The Stats tab helped him visualize all this, track goal completion rates, and understand his time distribution across different practice categories, making lesson planning easier for his teacher.
